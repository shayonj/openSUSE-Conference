Location

The openSUSE Conference will be held October 20-23 in beautiful city of Prague in the Czech republic. If you require special assistance, please indicate when you register for the Conference

Our venue is the Czech Technical University in Prague.



The conference will be held at Faculty of Information Technology
of Czech Technical University in Prague (FIT ČVUT). More precisely in the new building at Thákurova 9, Prague 6 – Dejvice.


Talks are held at the ground floor in halls 105 (Linux Days) and 155 (openSUSE Conference) and at third floor in room 349 (Gentoo Conference). Workshops are held in third floor as well. Booths of open source project can be find in front of halls at the ground level.

How to get to the venue?

If you are traveling by public transport, closest stops are tram stops Lotyšská (from east, tram 8 and 2) and Thákurova (from south, tram 20 and 26). End station of underground A Dejvická is also close by (cca 600 m away). You can use web of Prague public transport services to find out how to get there.

By train

If you are coming by train to the Main station, you can either take tram number 26 and exit at tram stop Thákurova, or take underground C in direction of Háje, but exit at Muzeum station, change the lines there and use underground A to the station Dejvická. Both variants takes approximately same time.

By bus

If you are coming by bus and you are arriving at Florenc station, you can either take tram number 8 and exit at tram stop Lotyšská, or take underground C in direction of Háje, but exit at Muzeum station, change the lines there and use underground A to the station Dejvická. Both variants takes approximately same time.

By plane

Prague is served by a single international airport, the Prague Ruzyně International Airport, airport code PRG. The airport is served by many international carriers both inside and outside Europe. The Czech Republic is part of the Schengen zone, so travellers within the zone does not need immigration processing.

If you are coming by plane to the Praha-Ruzyně airport, take bus 119 and exit at station Dejvická.

About Prague:

Everybody knows Prague is a beautiful city. It is the capital of the Czech Republic and a major European metropole. With about 2 million people in the area, it is the fourteenth largest city in the EU and is over a 1000 years old. In its history it has long been the political, cultural, and economic centre of central Europe and much of this is still visible in the beautiful buildings and the many museums.

Below is a list of some of the main attractions in Prague. Most of them are close by, all of them can be easily reached by the excellent public transport in Prague.

  • Prague Castle
  • Charles Bridge
  • Old Town Square
  • The Jewish Quarter
  • The Lennon Wall
  • Petřín hill
