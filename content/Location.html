Location

The openSUSE Conference will be held July 19 until July 22 2013, hosted by Thessaloniki, the second largest city in Greece and home to no less than 15 UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Hundreds of hackers from around the globe will come to the country where the modern Age of Reason started. In the country where over 2500 years ago great thinkers like Parmenides, Hippocrates and Socrates sat down and taught the art of logical and critical thinking, we'll now sit down and talk critically about logic and code. If you want to join us, discuss the mysteries of reality, existence, freedom and computer languages, work on the future of Free and Open Source Software, come and visit our event!

In the coming weeks, content about Thessaliniki, Greece and the Greek Free and Open Source community will be added here.

About Prague:

Everybody knows Prague is a beautiful city. It is the capital of the Czech Republic and a major European metropole. With about 2 million people in the area, it is the fourteenth largest city in the EU and is over a 1000 years old. In its history it has long been the political, cultural, and economic centre of central Europe and much of this is still visible in the beautiful buildings and the many museums.

Below is a list of some of the main attractions in Prague. Most of them are close by, all of them can be easily reached by the excellent public transport in Prague.

  • Prague Castle
  • Charles Bridge
  • Old Town Square
  • The Jewish Quarter
  • The Lennon Wall
  • Petřín hill
